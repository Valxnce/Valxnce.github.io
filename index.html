About Vortex Lab

Vortex Lab is a center for experimental and theoretical physics focused on fundamental questions: how particles interact, how spacetime behaves near singularities, and how quantum information can be harnessed for sensing and computation. We publish datasets, open-source software, and hardware designs to accelerate reproducible research.

Instrumentation

Design and build detectors, amplifiers, and cryogenic apparatus for precision measurements at low energies and in high-energy collisions.

Theory

Develop models for quantum gravity, black hole interiors, and non-perturbative field theory. Emphasis on testable predictions for next-generation experiments.

Data & Software

Open analysis pipelines, simulation stacks, and reproducible notebooks for data from detectors and simulations.

Research Areas

Our research bridges experiment and theory. Below are core themes where we currently invest our efforts.

Quantum Sensors

Atomic magnetometers, optomechanical accelerometers, and squeezed-light interferometry used to probe weak signals from fundamental physics and geodesy.

High-Energy Instrumentation

Detector geometries and read-out electronics for tracking, calorimetry, and timing at sub-nanosecond resolution.

Black Holes & Gravitation

Theoretical studies of horizons, quantum corrections, and potential observational signatures in gravitational waveforms.

Quantum Information

Entanglement structure in many-body systems, error-correcting codes inspired by holography, and quantum simulations of field theories.

Dark Matter Searches

Designing experiments sensitive to ultra-light axions, WIMPs, and other beyond-standard-model candidates using precision detectors.

Computational Physics

High-performance simulations, machine learning for event classification, and data-driven model discovery.

Projects & Instrumentation

Selected active and archival projects across hardware, software, and theory.

Quantum Interferometer V2

High-precision interferometer using squeezed light and active stabilization; deployed in lab for gravitational sensing tests.

Low-Background Detector Array

Modular detectors for low-count-rate experiments with active veto and radon suppression systems.

Accelerator Diagnostics Suite

Real-time beam instrumentation for emittance and profile measurements at high repetition rates.

Dark Matter Haloscope

Tunable cavity and SQUID-based readout for axion searches in the microwave band.

High-Throughput Simulation

Cluster-managed workflows for lattice field theory and event generation with automated analysis.

Detector-Sim Suite

Open-source toolkit for simulating detector responses and digitization chains.

Quantum Wormhole Modeling

Exploratory simulations of wormhole traversability under quantum-corrected backgrounds and energy conditions.

Neutrino Prototype

Compact array for low-energy neutrino detection using novel scintillators and machine learning triggers.

Holographic Codes

Algorithmic development for error-correcting codes inspired by AdS/CFT and tensor network constructions.

Cryogenics 3.0

Reliable cryocooler integration and vibration isolation for low-noise experiments.

Time-of-flight Array

Precision timing array optimized for cosmic ray and particle identification tasks.

Open Data Portal

Publishing cleaned datasets with DOIs, Jupyter notebooks, and analysis recipes.

AI Physics Assistant

Model-assisted literature reviews and parameter scans for experimental design.

Education Outreach

Workshops, lectures, and open resources for students and early-career researchers.

Quantum Control Stack

Hardware and firmware to control qubit-like sensors and their readout chains.
